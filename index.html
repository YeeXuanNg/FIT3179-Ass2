Malaysia’s Water Usage and Supply: Trends from 2020 to 2022

Overview

From 2020 to 2022, Malaysia’s water supply consistently exceeded demand, with total production rising from 17,928 to 18,828 million liters per day (MLD). Domestic water consumption peaked in 2021 before slightly declining in 2022, while non-domestic consumption fluctuated over the same period. These national trends provide context for understanding how water demand and supply vary across states.

After examining rainfall patterns, raw water extraction, and source composition, we now turn to water treatment plants (WTPs) — the infrastructure that converts raw water into safe, usable supply for households and industries across Malaysia.

This bar chart shows the number of water treatment plants (WTPs) in each Malaysian state. States with more plants generally have higher treatment capacity, ensuring reliable water supply for both domestic and non-domestic users.

Conclusion

From 2020 to 2022, Malaysia’s water supply consistently exceeded demand, supported by rising production and well-distributed water treatment plants across states. Domestic and non-domestic consumption varied regionally, while raw water sources and annual rainfall ensured sufficient availability.

The interaction between rainfall, sources, production, and infrastructure highlights the importance of effective water resource management. Together, these insights illustrate how natural resources, production, infrastructure, and usage patterns interact to shape Malaysia’s water system, providing a basis for sustainable management and guiding future strategies.
